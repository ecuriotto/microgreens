How to grow Microgreens

Meet three people who could benefit greatly from incorporating microgreens into their diet:

Seeds

Choose your seeds wisely. Consider the variety of seeds available and choose those that you like or that are good for your health.

Medium

Prepare the growing medium. You can use soil or a growing mat as the growing medium. Make sure the medium is moist, but not too wet. Spread the seeds evenly on the surface, making sure that they are not too close together.

Germination

Germinate the seeds. Cover the seeds with another layer of growing mat or paper towels, and then place a weight on top of the mat or paper towels to ensure good seed-to-soil contact. Keep the seeds in a dark place for a few days to germinate.

Light

Provide light. After the seeds have germinated, expose the microgreens to light. You can use natural light or artificial light for this purpose. Place the microgreens in a warm and bright area, and water them regularly to keep them moist. Try to water them from the bottom to prevent mold.

Harvest

Harvest and enjoy. After a few days of exposure to light, the microgreens should be ready for harvest. Cut them off at the base and rinse them in cold water. Pat them dry and enjoy them in a salad, sandwich, or any other dish you like.
